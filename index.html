What is Dragon Ball

Dragon Ball is a legendary Japanese manga and anime franchise created by Akira Toriyama. It follows the z fighters who are very strong fighters who train, compete, and defend their world while chasing the mystical Dragon Balls seven powerful orbs that can summon a wish-granting dragon named Shenron. Over time, the story grows from an adventurous martial arts journey into a high stakes and action pack series know for memorable rivalries, intense battles, and iconic transformations.

The Main Series

Dragon Ball has multiple major series that build on the same universe in different ways. Each era has its own tone, focus, and style. Each ranging from adventure to comedy to world shaking action.

Dragon Ball (Original)

Dragon Ball begins with Goku as a child and focuses heavily on discovery, humor, martial arts training, and classic tournament arcs. It introduces the core cast, the concept of the Dragon Balls, and the early foundations of the world. If you want the fullest understanding of where everything started, this is the most complete beginning.

Dragon Ball Z

Dragon Ball Z is the series most associated with the franchise’s biggest moments. It escalates the stakes dramatically with powerful villains, major story arcs, and signature transformations such as the Super Sayian transformation. Z expands the universe, deepens rivalries, and defines what many know what “Dragon Ball” is.

Dragon Ball GT

Dragon Ball GT takes the story in a different direction and has its own identity compared to later modern entries. It aims for a return to adventure while still delivering major battles and new forms. GT is often viewed as an optional path but is well known for the iconic Super Sayian 4 transformation.

Dragon Ball Super

Dragon Ball Super continues the Dragon Ball universe with a modern style and introduces new cosmic-scale ideas such as gods, powerful new opponents, and the 12 universes of Dragon Ball. Super blends humor and everyday character moments with large scale arcs that push the franchise into bigger multiverse level storys.

Dragon Ball Daima

Dragon Ball Daima is the most recent entry in the franchise’s lineup. It leans into the classic adventure feeling of the orginal Dragon Ball series while still keeping the action and character chemistry that defines Dragon Ball. Daima is often discussed as a modern series that is great for the younger audience of Dragon Ball.

What people love about Dragon Ball

  • Progression: training and growth feeling earned
  • Rivalries: enemies can become allies over time such as Vegeta
  • Moments: emotional turning points during major battles and the iconic transformations

Where should new fans start?

A popular starting point is Dragon Ball Z because it contains many of the franchise’s most famous arcs and is the version most people think of when talking about Dragon Ball.

If you want a quicker introduction to the modern Dragon Ball feel the movie Dragon Ball Super: Broly is a strong option to understand the action style, characters, and energy that Dragon Ball brings when watching. Then you can choose whether to continue with Z for deeper context or explore Super afterward.

Beginner Paths

  1. Full context: Dragon Ball → Dragon Ball Z
  2. Most common: Dragon Ball Z → Dragon Ball Super
  3. Fast entry point: Dragon Ball Super: Broly → then Z or Super
